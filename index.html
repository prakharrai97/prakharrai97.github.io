§ 01 / About

A controls engineer who writes models, not just code.

BIO

I work on chassis controls in the Tier‑1 automotive industry — specifically, the actuators that will define how the next generation of vehicles stops and steers. My day‑to‑day lives in Simulink, C, and CAN traces; my research lives in physics‑informed neural networks, differentiable simulators, and learning‑based methods that still have to pass functional safety review.

I hold an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Michigan Technological University with a graduate certification in Control Systems, and I am an active inventor with two production‑deployed patents in EMB and EPS. I care about making virtual development actually predict physical hardware — and about building the calibration tooling that lets that claim be tested rigorously.

§ 02 / Focus

Where the research lives.

AREAS
01

Scientific Machine Learning

Physics‑Informed Neural Networks, Neural ODEs, and Universal Differential Equations applied to automotive actuator modeling — gray‑box architectures that respect first principles while learning the residual.

02

Digital Twins

Models of electromechanical brakes and EPS systems faithful enough for closed‑loop virtual calibration, stability analysis, and pre‑silicon controller validation.

03

Safe Learning Control

Control Barrier Functions, safe reinforcement learning, and Koopman operator methods — bringing data‑driven controllers into a domain where ASIL constraints and compute budgets are non‑negotiable.

04

Automated Calibration

Bayesian optimization and meta‑learning frameworks for cross‑platform tuning — collapsing calibration cycles that today take human engineers weeks of test‑track time.

§ 03 / Research

UPCOMING
SAE Brake Colloquium · 2026

A Physics‑Informed Neural Network Digital Twin for Electromechanical Brake Systems

Presents a gray‑box architecture that augments a Stribeck friction model with a learned neural residual, enabling accurate pad‑force estimation across the full range of EMB operating regimes. The twin is trained with curriculum learning over dynamic conditions and evaluated against production brake‑by‑wire hardware.
